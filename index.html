Overview

The League of Friends of the Blind (LOFOB) is an 85-year-old non-governmental organization (NGO) that serves the blind and visually impaired, and their families and caregivers, in the Western Cape, South Africa.

Our team was tasked with identifying opportunities to improve their communication stategy and information flow.

Role: UX Researcher and Designer | Time frame: March 2017 - July 2017 | Location: Cape Town, South Africa

problem

Our team's first goal was to review and revise the initial problem statement given to us by our client.

Discovery

My team and I had weekly calls with our client prior to our departure, which gave us a lot of important background information. However, we woun'dt be able to really truly understand the client's problem without examining current context and practices.

How can we leverage an organization’s 85-year-old history to improve communications, engagement and evaluation with clients, their families and caregivers, funders and other external stakeholders? How can we add value to and improve upon existing processes given organizational, resource and other constraints?

Our problem and objective

I participated in this project through the support of the University of Michigan School of Information Global Information Engagement Program. Each year, GIEP accepts up to 18 graduate students from across the University to consult, in small teams, a range of NGOs in Cape Town, South Africa. The entire program spans four months: a two-month graduate level seminar followed by a two-month placement on site.
